Starting Point

A business setup question with broader legal and commercial consequences

This matter involved structuring a healthcare platform across offshore, free zone, and mainland jurisdictions in the UAE. The objective was not limited to incorporation. The framework needed to protect intellectual property, preserve strategic control, create a clean entry point for future investors, and ensure regulatory compliance for the operating business.

The client required a legal structure that could support business growth over time, not just initial setup. That meant separating intellectual property from operational exposure, preserving control among founding stakeholders, and creating a structure that would remain workable when future investors entered the platform.

The Challenge

Balancing control, compliance, protection, and future scalability in one structure

The complexity of the matter lay in the fact that each priority affected the others. The legal design had to work as one coordinated system rather than a series of isolated setup decisions.

Protecting intellectual property from operational and financial exposure.

Investor Entry

Creating a clear pathway for future investor participation without compromising structure.

Stakeholder Control

Retaining strategic control with founding stakeholders.

Ensuring alignment with Dubai Healthcare Authority requirements.

Commercial Durability

Designing for long-term growth rather than only immediate setup convenience.

The Structure

Designing a four-tier framework separating ownership, control, investment, and operations

The final structure was built as a four-tier framework, with each entity serving a distinct legal and commercial purpose while maintaining overall cohesion and control.

01
JAFZA Offshore

IP Holding Entity

An offshore entity was established to hold the platform’s intellectual property, including brand assets and operational know-how.

  • Centralized IP ownership
  • Shielded core assets from operational exposure
  • Created flexibility for future licensing structures
02
Dubai Free Zone

Management Company

A management entity was structured with the client and core investors as shareholders, forming the control layer of the platform.

  • Retained strategic and operational control
  • Centralized governance and decision-making
  • Oversaw downstream group entities
03
Dubai Free Zone

Investment HoldCo

A wholly owned subsidiary was established to function as the investment vehicle for future participation.

  • Created a clean route for onboarding future investors
  • Preserved a simpler cap table structure
  • Separated investor exposure from the IP layer
04
Dubai Mainland

Operating Entity

The mainland company was set up as the licensed healthcare entity carrying out operational activities.

  • Supported DHA licensing requirements
  • Conducted revenue-generating operations
  • Ensured regulatory compliance at the operating level
Execution

From legal design to implementation across multiple jurisdictions

The work extended beyond high-level structuring. The engagement required coordinating the framework from concept through implementation across multiple legal and regulatory environments.

Framework Design

Designed the full multi-entity structure and mapped the relationship between ownership, management, investment, and operations.

Jurisdiction Selection

Advised on the role and suitability of offshore, free zone, and mainland vehicles within the wider framework.

Incorporation Coordination

Supported implementation across the relevant jurisdictions to align structure with execution.

Healthcare Regulatory Positioning

Managed the DHA licensing process and aligned the operating entity with applicable healthcare requirements.

Operational Readiness

Supported practical setup steps including banking and readiness for business activity.

Outcome

A structure built for compliance today and scalability tomorrow

The final result was more than a legally valid setup. It was a business-ready framework capable of supporting future investment, protecting core assets, and reducing structural friction as the platform grows.

Enabled scalable business growth and future investor participation
Achieved clear separation between intellectual property and operational exposure
Ensured alignment with healthcare regulatory requirements
Positioned the platform for long-term expansion with stronger control architecture
